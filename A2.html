The Three Virtues

Faith


Faith is being sure of what we hope for and certain of what we do not see.In Greek, the root word from which we get 'faith, the noun is PISTIS, and 'believe', the verb is PISTUEO.

Hope


“Hope” is commonly used to mean a wish : its strength is the strength of the person's desire. Hope is the confident expectation of what God has promised and its strength is in His faithfulness.

Love


Love is patient, love is kind. It does not envy, it does not boast, it is not proud.Four Greek words are used: Eros(for sensual or romantic love), Storge(family love), Philia(beloved,in a personal, intimate way), and Agape(unconditional "God" love).
